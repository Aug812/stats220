University of Auckland Gym Observation Project

Jiaen Wu

Introduction

The University of Aucklands gym activity is observed and reported in this data narrative. Over several days, I kept track of the activities, locations, and times spent at the gym. The goal is to investigate the most popular activities, the most popular gyms, and the times when individuals visit.

Observed Activity Types

This plot shows that strength training and cardio were the most popular observed activities.

Observations by Location

This plot shows that the UoA Recreation Centre was the most frequently visited gym.

Observation by Time Period

This plot shows that gym visits were most common in the evening, followed by afternoon and morning visits.

Conclusion

According to observational data, strength training and aerobic exercises are preferred by the majority of University of Auckland gym patrons. Due to its accessibility and cost for students, the UoA Recreation Centre is by far the most popular gym venue. Evenings see the highest gym attendance, suggesting that students prefer to work out after work or classes. This observational studys short time frame and failure to take seasonal or special event variations into account are two of its limitations. To have a better knowledge of gym usage behaviours, future research should look into demographic aspects like age and gender or further investigate patterns between weekdays and weekends.
